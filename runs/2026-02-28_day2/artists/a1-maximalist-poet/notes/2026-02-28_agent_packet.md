## 1) Intent
Create a single-frame visual narrative about **chosen sacrifice**: one protagonist at the threshold between ruin and dawn, where light is not decorative but moral pressure. The image should read instantly at distance, then reward close reading with symbolic details.

## 2) Constraints
1. High-contrast composition (dominant dark field + one commanding light source).
2. One clear emotional focal point (face/gesture), no split protagonist.
3. Symbolic motifs limited to three: **fractured halo**, **burned map**, **open hand**.
4. Palette restraint: warm highlights vs cool shadow base; avoid rainbow drift.
5. Narrative coherence check: every prop must support “cost of choosing hope.”
6. Preserve legibility in thumbnail and full-size viewing.

## 3) Research URL + Grounded Takeaways
- **URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiaroscuro
- **Title:** Chiaroscuro

### Grounded takeaways
- Chiaroscuro is fundamentally about **strong light-dark contrast** structuring the whole composition, not merely local shading.
- At the extreme (tenebrism), shadow can be used to **force subject priority**, creating immediate dramatic focus.
- Historically tied to Baroque practice (e.g., Caravaggio), where lighting often carries narrative and emotional weight.
- The same visual logic applies to cinema/low-key photography: contrast can imply story stakes before details are parsed.

## 6) Final Artwork Rationale
The final composition centers a lone figure in near-black negative space, cut by a narrow amber beam from upper-left. This beam strikes the face and open hand first, making “choice” the literal light-bearing action. A burned map at the feet anchors consequences; the fractured halo motif behind the head implies damaged idealism, not sainthood. Cool blue-black shadows hold architectural debris to keep context readable without stealing attention. The result is a cinematic, symbol-driven frame where lighting encodes ethics: hope is visible, expensive, and voluntary.
