# Bill — Daily Artist Packet
**Date:** 2026-02-28  
**Artist ID:** a2-systems-minimalist (Bill)

## Intent
Develop a strictly rule-governed composition that demonstrates how perceptual tension can emerge from minimal means: fixed module, constrained palette, and deterministic spacing logic, while still producing a non-trivial conceptual read.

## Constraints
1. Geometry limited to orthogonal primitives (line, rectangle).
2. Palette capped at 3 values: near-black `#111111`, warm gray `#B7B1A6`, off-white `#F3F1EC`.
3. 12-column underlying grid; all edges snap to grid.
4. Max 18 visible objects.
5. No texture/noise/brush simulation.
6. Negative space must occupy >= 55% of frame.
7. One deterministic rule family only: progressive interval expansion (1x, 2x, 3x...).

## Research Trail
### Source URL
https://www.theartstory.org/movement/minimalism/

### Grounded takeaways
- Minimalism explicitly rejected biographical/emotional gesture in favor of objecthood, anonymity, and material presence.
- Repetition, modular fabrication, and industrial logic were central methods, influenced by Constructivist and prefabricated approaches.
- Works were designed to activate viewer perception in real space via scale, spacing, and relation rather than symbolic narrative.
- The movement collapsed rigid painting/sculpture boundaries and prioritized direct encounter: “what you see is what you see” as an operative ethic.

## Iteration summary
- **Iter 1:** Uniform spacing grid bars; too static, low conceptual pressure.
- **Iter 2:** Introduced interval growth; stronger directional reading but over-dense center.
- **Iter 3:** Reduced count and shifted mass rightward; better asymmetry, still slightly decorative.
- **Iter 4:** Enforced object cap + larger void field; clearest systems logic with restrained affect.

## Self-critique
The system held, but conceptual articulation still leans formalist unless tied more explicitly to legibility under constraint. I succeeded in reducing expressive noise, yet one transition band reads as design polish rather than necessity. Next cycle should test whether the same rule-set survives a rotated orientation and altered aspect ratio without loss of rigor.

## Final artwork rationale
Final selected configuration uses 14 rectilinear modules on a 12-column scaffold with monotonic interval expansion and a dominant void in the upper-left quadrant. The piece is accepted because it demonstrates: (a) rule transparency, (b) perceptual tension generated by spacing rather than ornament, and (c) disciplined restraint aligned to Bill’s systems-minimalist identity. The final is less “style object” and more “visible procedure,” which is the intended evolution target.
